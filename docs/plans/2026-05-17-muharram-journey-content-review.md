## Day 1 — Awakening to the Cause of Husayn (AS)

- **Theme (AR):** اليَقَظَةُ لِقَضِيَّةِ الحُسَيْنِ عَلَيْهِ السَّلام
- **Icon:** `moon.zzz.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ، رِضَا اللَّهِ رِضَانَا أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ، نَصْبِرُ عَلَىٰ بَلَائِهِ وَيُوَفِّينَا أُجُورَ الصَّابِرِينَ
- **Transliteration:** Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un. Rida-llahi ridana ahla-l-bayt, nasbiru 'ala bala'ihi wa yuwaffina ujura-s-sabirin
- **English:** Indeed we belong to Allah and to Him we shall return. The pleasure of Allah is our pleasure, the People of the House; we are patient through His trial, and He will pay us in full the reward of the patient.
- **Source:** Words of Imam al-Husayn (AS) on departing for Karbala — *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus); the opening clause is **Quran 2:156**.
- ⚠️ The "riḍā Allāhi riḍānā ahl al-bayt…" sentence is a well-known saying transmitted from Imam al-Husayn (AS) in the maqtal/ziyarat tradition; the *exact* wording varies slightly across maqtal prints. The Quranic clause (2:156) is exact.

**Verses:**
- **2:156** — "Indeed we belong to Allah and to Him we return" — the istirja' the believer turns to in calamity; the gateway phrase of all azadari for Imam al-Husayn (AS).
- **89:27** — "O reassured soul, return to your Lord" — the verse Shia tradition reads upon Imam al-Husayn (AS); awakening to his cause begins that returning soul's journey.
- **2:45** — "Seek help through patience and prayer" — the posture for entering the ten days of mourning.

**Tafsir focus:** Muharram opens not with celebration but with a turning of the heart. The istirja' of 2:156 is not mere resignation; in the tafsir of grief it is the soul recognising that everything, even the beloved Imam (AS), belongs to Allah and returns to Him. Husayn (AS) himself spoke these words as he set out, accepting Allah's decree with sabr. To 'awaken' to his cause is to let his sacrifice pierce a heart that had grown comfortable, and to enter these days seeking Allah through patience and prayer.

**Reflection:** Before the mourning begins, sit in silence. Has the tragedy of Husayn (AS) become a familiar story you no longer feel? Ask Allah to reopen this wound in your heart.

## Day 2 — Truth Over Comfort: The Refusal to Pledge to Yazid

- **Theme (AR):** الحَقُّ فَوْقَ الرَّاحَة
- **Icon:** `hand.raised.slash.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** إِنَّا أَهْلُ بَيْتِ النُّبُوَّةِ، وَمَعْدِنُ الرِّسَالَةِ، وَمُخْتَلَفُ الْمَلَائِكَةِ، وَيَزِيدُ رَجُلٌ فَاسِقٌ، وَمِثْلِي لَا يُبَايِعُ مِثْلَهُ
- **Transliteration:** Inna ahlu bayti-n-nubuwwah, wa ma'dinu-r-risalah, wa mukhtalafu-l-mala'ikah, wa Yazidu rajulun fasiq, wa mithli la yubayi'u mithlah
- **English:** We are the household of prophethood, the substance of the message, and those whom the angels visit; and Yazid is a corrupt man — a man like me does not pledge allegiance to a man like him.
- **Source:** Reply of Imam al-Husayn (AS) to al-Walid ibn Utba in Medina — *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus); *Maqtal al-Husayn* (al-Muqarram).
- This statement (incl. "mithlī lā yubāyi'u mithlah") is well attested across the maqtal corpus (al-Lohoof, al-Muqarram citing al-Khwarizmi / al-Tabari / al-Irshad). Verified against al-Islam.org's al-Qurashi *Life of Imam Husayn* and al-Muqarram.

**Verses:**
- **2:42** — "Do not mix truth with falsehood, nor conceal the truth while you know it" — Husayn (AS) would not lend false legitimacy to Yazid.
- **11:113** — "Do not incline toward those who do wrong, lest the Fire touch you" — the refusal of bay'ah as obedience to this command.
- **9:24** — If wealth, kin and dwellings are more beloved than Allah and His cause, await His decree — Husayn (AS) chose Allah's cause over comfort.

**Tafsir focus:** When al-Walid summoned him to pledge allegiance, Imam al-Husayn (AS) did not weigh his life against the truth. The Quran forbids cloaking falsehood in the garment of truth (2:42) and forbids leaning toward oppressors (11:113). Yazid's rule was open corruption; to pledge to it was to conceal a truth that the household of prophethood was created to guard. The lens here is the cost of honesty: faith sometimes demands that comfort, safety and even life be set beneath the truth.

**Reflection:** Where in your own life do you stay silent for comfort when the truth needs a voice? Husayn (AS) refused with everything to lose. What do you refuse to risk?

## Day 3 — Migration for Principle: Medina → Mecca → Iraq

- **Theme (AR):** الهِجْرَةُ مِنْ أَجْلِ المَبْدَأ
- **Icon:** `figure.walk.departure`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** إِنِّي ذَاهِبٌ إِلَىٰ رَبِّي سَيَهْدِينِ، رَبِّ نَجِّنِي مِنَ الْقَوْمِ الظَّالِمِينَ
- **Transliteration:** Inni dhahibun ila rabbi sayahdin. Rabbi najjini mina-l-qawmi-z-zalimin
- **English:** Indeed I am going to my Lord; He will guide me. My Lord, deliver me from the wrongdoing people.
- **Source:** **Quran 37:99** (words of Prophet Ibrahim AS on his hijrah) and **Quran 28:21** (words of Prophet Musa AS leaving Egypt) — Quranic supplications of departure for the sake of Allah, applied as the spirit of Husayn's (AS) journey.
- This entry deliberately uses two verbatim Quranic supplications (not a maqtal quotation) so the dua wording is exact and unimpeachable; they are presented as the *spirit* of his migration, not as words attributed to him.

**Verses:**
- **28:21** — "My Lord, deliver me from the wrongdoing people" — Musa's (AS) prayer leaving Egypt; Husayn (AS) likewise left rather than submit to wrong.
- **37:99** — "Indeed I am going to my Lord; He will guide me" — Ibrahim's (AS) words as he migrated for his Lord.
- **29:26** — "Indeed I will emigrate to my Lord" — migration in the Quran as movement for principle, not escape.

**Tafsir focus:** Imam al-Husayn (AS) left Medina near the end of Rajab, reached Mecca, and then set out for Iraq rather than give his hand to Yazid. The Quran frames the migrations of Ibrahim and Musa (AS) not as flight but as deliberate movement toward Allah and away from oppression (37:99, 28:21, 29:26). Husayn's (AS) journey is read in the same light: every stage was a refusal to let principle be buried for safety. The tafsir lens is hijrah as an act of the heart before it is a movement of the body.

**Reflection:** Husayn (AS) left the city of his grandfather ﷺ rather than betray a principle. What comfort or place would you have to leave to keep your faith whole?

> Note on facts: timeline (Medina → Mecca, then toward Iraq, leaving Medina around the end of Rajab) verified via al-Islam.org Maqtal materials and Wikipedia *Battle of Karbala*. No specific maqtal *quotation* is attributed here, so there is no wording risk.

## Day 4 — Loyalty & True Companionship: The Ansar of Husayn (AS)

- **Theme (AR):** الوَفَاءُ وَصِدْقُ الصُّحْبَة
- **Icon:** `person.3.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ يَا أَنْصَارَ اللَّهِ وَأَنْصَارَ رَسُولِهِ وَأَنْصَارَ عَلِيِّ بْنِ أَبِي طَالِبٍ، وَأَنْصَارَ الْحَسَنِ، وَأَنْصَارَ الْحُسَيْنِ صَلَوَاتُ اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِمْ
- **Transliteration:** As-salamu 'alaykum ya ansara-llahi wa ansara rasulih, wa ansara 'Aliyyi-bni Abi Talib, wa ansara-l-Hasan, wa ansara-l-Husayn salawatu-llahi 'alayhim
- **English:** Peace be upon you, O helpers of Allah and helpers of His Messenger, and helpers of Ali ibn Abi Talib, and helpers of al-Hasan, and helpers of al-Husayn — Allah's blessings be upon them.
- **Source:** *Ziyarat al-Shuhada* (the martyrs of Karbala) — **Mafatih al-Jinan** (Shaykh Abbas Qummi).
- ⚠️ This is the opening salutation of Ziyarat al-Shuhada as it appears in Mafatih al-Jinan. Owner: confirm the exact list/order of names against your Mafatih copy if you wish it verbatim (the salutation to the "anṣār" of Allah, His Messenger, Ali, al-Hasan and al-Husayn is the standard opening).

**Verses:**
- **33:23** — "Among the believers are men true to what they pledged to Allah" — recited in Ziyarat for the loyal companions who fulfilled their covenant unto death.
- **48:29** — "Those with him are firm against the disbelievers, merciful among themselves" — a portrait of Husayn's (AS) Ansar on the night and morning of Ashura.
- **9:111** — "Allah has purchased from the believers their lives in return for Paradise" — the companions gave their souls in that very transaction.

**Tafsir focus:** The companions of Imam al-Husayn (AS) are remembered in Ziyarat with the words of 33:23 — believers true to what they pledged to Allah. Few in number, they would not abandon the grandson of the Prophet ﷺ. The Quran's image of the firm and merciful believers (48:29) and of the soul sold to Allah for Paradise (9:111) is fulfilled in them. The lens here is loyalty: true companionship is proven not in ease but at the moment when leaving would have been easy and forgivable, and they stayed.

**Reflection:** Husayn's (AS) companions could have walked away in the dark and been blameless. They chose him. To whom, or to what truth, is your loyalty tested when leaving would be easy?

## Day 5 — Patience Under Trial: The Water Cut Off

- **Theme (AR):** الصَّبْرُ عِنْدَ البَلَاء
- **Icon:** `drop.triangle.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** صَبْرًا عَلَىٰ قَضَائِكَ يَا رَبِّ، لَا إِلَٰهَ سِوَاكَ، يَا غِيَاثَ الْمُسْتَغِيثِينَ، مَا لِي رَبٌّ سِوَاكَ، وَلَا مَعْبُودَ غَيْرُكَ
- **Transliteration:** Sabran 'ala qada'ika ya rabb, la ilaha siwak, ya ghiyatha-l-mustaghithin, ma li rabbun siwak, wa la ma'buda ghayruk
- **English:** Patience in the face of Your decree, O my Lord; there is no god but You, O Helper of those who cry for help. I have no Lord but You, and none worshipped besides You.
- **Source:** **Verbatim** supplication of Imam al-Husayn (AS) on the day of Ashura — *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus); *Maqtal al-Husayn* (al-Muqarram, p. 283); *Bihar al-Anwar* (al-Majlisi).
- ✅ **Replaced with verbatim text per owner decision (2026-05-17).** This is the opening of the single attested supplication Imam al-Husayn (AS) raised on the day of Ashura ("ṣabran 'alā qaḍā'ika yā rabb, lā ilāha siwāk, yā ghiyātha-l-mustaghīthīn, mā lī rabbun siwāk, wa lā ma'būda ghayruk…"), recorded in al-Lohoof and Bihar al-Anwar and reproduced in al-Muqarram's *Maqtal al-Husayn*. It is **not** a composition. Verified verbatim against [imamhussain.org/arabic/7547](https://imamhussain.org/arabic/7547) and cross-checked against al-Muqarram's *Maqtal al-Husayn* on al-islam.org ("Grant me patience to bear Your destiny, O Lord! There is no god but You! O Helper of those who seek help!").

**Verses:**
- **2:155** — "We will surely test you with fear, hunger and loss… and give glad tidings to the patient" — the camp of Husayn (AS) endured exactly this.
- **2:153** — "Seek help through patience and prayer; indeed Allah is with the patient" — the inner provision when every outer provision was denied.
- **39:10** — "The patient will be given their reward without measure" — the immeasurable station earned through the thirst borne for Allah.

**Tafsir focus:** From the seventh of Muharram, Umar ibn Sa'd cut the water of the Euphrates from the camp of Imam al-Husayn (AS); the children and the companions thirsted. The Quran promises that the believers will be tested with fear, hunger and loss, and that glad tidings belong to the patient (2:155). Sabr in the Quran is not passive endurance but an active turning to Allah while the trial burns (2:153). The lens is patience as worship: the thirst of Karbala was borne not in despair but in submission, and its reward is 'without measure' (39:10).

**Reflection:** Recall the thirst of the children of Husayn (AS) before you drink today. When trial withholds what you need, do you turn to Allah with sabr, or only with complaint?

> Note on facts: water cut off by **Umar ibn Sa'd** ahead of the battle (al-Lohoof states the siege/water blockade; commonly placed on **7 Muharram**) — verified via al-Islam.org *Lohoof* page.

## Day 6 — Dignity Over Humiliation: Hayhāt minna-dh-dhilla

- **Theme (AR):** العِزَّةُ لَا الذِّلَّة — هَيْهَاتَ مِنَّا الذِّلَّة
- **Icon:** `flag.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** وَهَيْهَاتَ مِنَّا الذِّلَّةُ، يَأْبَى اللَّهُ لَنَا ذَٰلِكَ وَرَسُولُهُ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ، وَحُجُورٌ طَابَتْ وَطَهُرَتْ، وَأُنُوفٌ حَمِيَّةٌ، وَنُفُوسٌ أَبِيَّةٌ، مِنْ أَنْ تُؤْثِرَ طَاعَةَ اللِّئَامِ عَلَىٰ مَصَارِعِ الْكِرَامِ
- **Transliteration:** Wa hayhata minna-dh-dhillah, ya'ba-llahu lana dhalika wa rasuluhu wa-l-mu'minun, wa hujurun tabat wa tahurat, wa anufun hamiyyah, wa nufusun abiyyah, min an tu'thira ta'ata-l-li'ami 'ala masari'i-l-kiram
- **English:** Far, far from us is humiliation. Allah refuses that for us, and His Messenger, and the believers, and laps that were pure and chaste, and proud souls that disdain to prefer the obedience of the vile over the noble death of the honourable.
- **Source:** Ashura sermon of Imam al-Husayn (AS) — *Tuhaf al-Uqul* (Ibn Shu'ba al-Harrani); also *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus).
- This is the well-known passage; Arabic verified against wikishia "Hayhat minna al-dhilla" (which cites *Tuhaf al-'uqul*). High confidence on wording.

**Verses:**
- **63:8** — "To Allah belongs all honour, and to His Messenger and the believers" — the very source of the dignity Husayn (AS) refused to surrender.
- **3:139** — "Do not lose heart nor grieve, for you are the higher if you are believers" — the elevation humiliation cannot reach.
- **4:139** — Honour belongs entirely to Allah — so it cannot be sought from, nor lost to, a tyrant like Yazid.

**Tafsir focus:** On the day of Ashura, Imam al-Husayn (AS) declared 'hayhāt minna-dh-dhilla' — humiliation is far from us. The Quran roots all 'izza, all honour, in Allah, His Messenger and the believers (63:8), and tells the believers they are the higher ones (3:139). Honour sought from a tyrant is no honour at all (4:139). The lens is the Quranic meaning of dignity: it is not pride of self but refusal to bow the soul to anyone but Allah, even when the cost is the 'noble death of the honourable'.

**Reflection:** Husayn (AS) would die rather than live one breath in humiliation before falsehood. What small humiliations of conscience do you accept for an easier life?

## Day 7 — Sacrifice: The Offering of Sons and Brothers

- **Theme (AR):** التَّضْحِيَةُ بِالأَبْنَاءِ وَالإِخْوَة
- **Icon:** `drop.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** قَتَلَ اللَّهُ قَوْمًا قَتَلُوكَ، مَا أَجْرَأَهُمْ عَلَى اللَّهِ وَعَلَىٰ انْتِهَاكِ حُرْمَةِ الرَّسُولِ، عَلَى الدُّنْيَا بَعْدَكَ الْعَفَاءُ
- **Transliteration:** Qatala-llahu qawman qataluk, ma ajra'ahum 'ala-llahi wa 'ala-ntihaki hurmati-r-rasul, 'ala-d-dunya ba'daka-l-'afa'
- **English:** May Allah slay a people who slew you. How bold they were against Allah and against the violation of the sanctity of the Messenger! Upon the world, after you, be desolation.
- **Source:** **Verbatim** words of Imam al-Husayn (AS) at the martyrdom of his son Ali al-Akbar — *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus, p. 67); also *Bihar al-Anwar* (al-Majlisi, vol. 45, p. 65) and *al-Irshad* (Shaykh al-Mufid, vol. 2, p. 106).
- ✅ **Replaced with verbatim text per owner decision (2026-05-17).** This is the single attested sentence Imam al-Husayn (AS) spoke as he stood over the body of his son Ali al-Akbar — the offering of a son at Karbala, exactly the Day 7 theme. It is **not** a composition. Verified verbatim against [alhodacenter.com/article/2265](https://alhodacenter.com/article/2265) (which quotes *al-Lohoof*, Ibn Tawus, p. 67 verbatim) and cross-checked against al-Muqarram's *Maqtal al-Husayn* on al-islam.org ("There is no good in life after you…"); the same wording is given in Bihar al-Anwar (45:65), al-Irshad (2:106), I'lam al-Wara (1:464) and Manaqib Ibn Shahr Ashub (3:257).

**Verses:**
- **37:107** — "And We ransomed him with a great sacrifice" — read in Shia tradition as foreshadowing the supreme sacrifice fulfilled by Husayn (AS).
- **2:154** — "Do not say of those slain in the way of Allah that they are dead; rather they are alive" — the sons and brothers offered at Karbala live with their Lord.
- **3:169** — "Those slain in the way of Allah are alive with their Lord, receiving provision" — the station of Ali al-Akbar, al-Abbas and the household.

**Tafsir focus:** Imam al-Husayn (AS) offered his sons, his brothers and his companions one by one — Ali al-Akbar, the infant Ali al-Asghar, his brother al-Abbas. The Quran calls Ibrahim's offering a 'great sacrifice' (37:107); the maqtal tradition sees its true fulfilment at Karbala. Yet the Quran insists the slain in Allah's way are not dead but living, provided for by their Lord (2:154, 3:169). The lens is sacrifice as transaction with Allah: what was given at Karbala was not lost but transformed into eternal life.

**Reflection:** Husayn (AS) buried his own sons for the sake of Allah's religion. Hold that grief for a moment. What do you cling to that you would not give for Allah?

## Day 8 — Steadfastness of the Women: Sayyida Zaynab (AS)

- **Theme (AR):** ثَبَاتُ النِّسَاء — السَّيِّدَةُ زَيْنَبُ عَلَيْهَا السَّلام
- **Icon:** `shield.lefthalf.filled`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** مَا رَأَيْتُ إِلَّا جَمِيلًا، هَٰؤُلَاءِ قَوْمٌ كَتَبَ اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِمُ الْقَتْلَ فَبَرَزُوا إِلَىٰ مَضَاجِعِهِمْ، وَسَيَجْمَعُ اللَّهُ بَيْنَكَ وَبَيْنَهُمْ فَتُحَاجُّ وَتُخَاصَمُ
- **Transliteration:** Ma ra'aytu illa jamila. Ha'ula'i qawmun kataba-llahu 'alayhimu-l-qatla fa-barazu ila madaji'ihim, wa sa-yajma'u-llahu baynaka wa baynahum fa-tuhajju wa tukhasam
- **English:** I saw nothing but beauty. These were a people for whom Allah had decreed martyrdom, so they went forth to their resting places; and Allah will gather you and them, and you will be questioned and disputed against.
- **Source:** Reply of Sayyida Zaynab (AS) to Yazid in his court — *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus); *Nafasul Mahmoom* (Shaykh Abbas Qummi).
- "Mā ra'aytu illā jamīlā" is among the most widely transmitted lines of the Karbala aftermath (her reply when Yazid asked how she found Allah's treatment of her family). Verified via wikishia "Sermon of Lady Zaynab (a) in Damascus". The continuation ("hā'ulā'i qawmun…") is the standard transmitted completion.

**Verses:**
- **3:173** — "Sufficient for us is Allah, and He is the best Disposer of affairs" — the unbroken faith of Sayyida Zaynab (AS) before Ibn Ziyad and Yazid.
- **14:27** — "Allah keeps firm those who believe with the firm word" — the divinely-given steadfastness that held her upright through Karbala, Kufa and Sham.
- **13:24** — "Peace be upon you for what you patiently endured" — the angels' salutation, fitting for the patience of the women of Karbala.

**Tafsir focus:** After the slaughter, the women and children were taken captive to Kufa and Sham. When Yazid mocked her, Sayyida Zaynab (AS) answered, 'I saw nothing but beauty', and delivered a sermon that shook his court. The Quran's words 'sufficient for us is Allah' (3:173) and 'Allah keeps firm those who believe' (14:27) describe her exactly: a faith that captivity could not bend. The lens is steadfastness — the women of Karbala carried the message of Husayn (AS) when the men had been martyred, and their patience earns the angels' greeting of peace (13:24).

**Reflection:** Zaynab (AS) stood before the killer of her brother and called the decree of Allah beautiful. When grief and injustice strike together, can your faith still say 'sufficient for us is Allah'?

## Day 9 — The Night of Ashura: Worship and Resolve

- **Theme (AR):** لَيْلَةُ عَاشُورَاء — العِبَادَةُ وَالعَزْم
- **Icon:** `moon.stars.fill`

**Dua / words:**
- **Arabic:** أُثْنِي عَلَى اللَّهِ أَحْسَنَ الثَّنَاءِ، أَمَّا بَعْدُ، فَإِنِّي لَا أَعْلَمُ أَصْحَابًا أَوْفَىٰ وَلَا خَيْرًا مِنْ أَصْحَابِي، وَلَا أَهْلَ بَيْتٍ أَبَرَّ وَلَا أَوْصَلَ مِنْ أَهْلِ بَيْتِي، فَجَزَاكُمُ اللَّهُ عَنِّي خَيْرًا
- **Transliteration:** Uthni 'ala-llahi ahsana-th-thana'. Amma ba'd, fa-inni la a'lamu as-haban awfa wa la khayran min as-habi, wa la ahla baytin abarra wa la awsala min ahli bayti, fa-jazakumu-llahu 'anni khayra
- **English:** I praise Allah with the most beautiful praise. As to what follows: I know of no companions more loyal or better than my companions, nor of a household more dutiful or more devoted than my household; may Allah reward you well on my behalf.
- **Source:** Address of Imam al-Husayn (AS) to his companions on the night of Ashura — *al-Lohoof* (Sayyid ibn Tawus).
- Faithful rendering of the Layla-Ashura address; verified in substance against al-Islam.org *Lohoof* ("The Battle and Its Related Events": "I do not find companions better than you, nor any family more righteous than my family"). Minor wording differs across translations of al-Lohoof; meaning is exact.

**Verses:**
- **51:17** — "They used to sleep but little of the night" — the night of Ashura, spent in prayer like the buzzing of bees.
- **25:64** — "Those who pass the night before their Lord prostrating and standing" — the portrait of the companions on Layla Ashura.
- **73:8** — "Remember the name of your Lord and devote yourself to Him completely" — the resolve with which they prepared to meet Allah.

**Tafsir focus:** On the eve of Ashura, Imam al-Husayn (AS) gathered his people, praised Allah, lifted his obligation from them and bade them leave under the cover of night — and not one departed. They then passed the night in worship, the sound of their ruku' and sujud like the humming of bees. The Quran praises those who sleep little of the night and stand before their Lord (51:17, 25:64) and calls the believer to devote himself wholly to Allah (73:8). The lens is worship as the source of resolve: the steadfastness of Ashura morning was forged in the prayers of Ashura night.

**Reflection:** They knew they would die at dawn, and they spent the night in prayer, not in fear. If you knew this were your last night, how would you spend it — and what stops you tonight?

> Note on facts: Imam (AS) released companions on Ashura eve & none left; night spent in worship "like the buzzing of bees" — both verified verbatim-in-substance via al-Islam.org *Lohoof*.

## Day 10 — Ashura: The Supreme Sacrifice and Its Eternal Message

- **Theme (AR):** عَاشُورَاء — التَّضْحِيَةُ الكُبْرَىٰ وَرِسَالَتُهَا الخَالِدَة
- **Icon:** `flame.fill`
- **This is the summit of grief — the somber emotional peak of the journey.**

**Dua / words (Ziyarat Ashura — opening salutation + concluding supplication):**
- **Arabic:** السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ يَا أَبَا عَبْدِ اللَّهِ وَعَلَى الْأَرْوَاحِ الَّتِي حَلَّتْ بِفِنَائِكَ، اللَّهُمَّ ارْزُقْنِي شَفَاعَةَ الْحُسَيْنِ يَوْمَ الْوُرُودِ، وَثَبِّتْ لِي قَدَمَ صِدْقٍ عِنْدَكَ مَعَ الْحُسَيْنِ وَأَصْحَابِ الْحُسَيْنِ الَّذِينَ بَذَلُوا مُهَجَهُمْ دُونَ الْحُسَيْنِ عَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ
- **Transliteration:** As-salamu 'alayka ya Aba 'Abdillah wa 'ala-l-arwahi-llati hallat bi-fina'ik. Allahumma-rzuqni shafa'ata-l-Husayni yawma-l-wurud, wa thabbit li qadama sidqin 'indaka ma'a-l-Husayni wa as-habi-l-Husayni-lladhina badhalu muhajahum duna-l-Husayni 'alayhi-s-salam
- **English:** Peace be upon you, O Aba Abdillah, and upon the souls that gathered at your courtyard. O Allah, grant me the intercession of al-Husayn on the Day of Arrival, and grant me a truthful standing with You alongside al-Husayn and the companions of al-Husayn — those who gave their lifeblood for al-Husayn, peace be upon him.
- **Source:** *Ziyarat Ashura* — opening salutation and concluding supplication — **Mafatih al-Jinan** (Shaykh Abbas Qummi).
- **High confidence.** Both segments verified verbatim against al-Islam.org's *The Torch of Perpetual Guidance* (Arabic + English of Ziyarat Ashura). The opening "as-salāmu 'alayka yā Abā 'Abdillāh wa 'ala-l-arwāḥi-llatī ḥallat bi-finā'ik" and the closing "Allāhumma-rzuqnī shafā'ata-l-Ḥusayn… alladhīna badhalū muhajahum dūna-l-Ḥusayn" are the canonical Ziyarat Ashura wording.

**Verses:**
- **89:27** — "O reassured soul, return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing" — the verses Shia tradition addresses to Imam al-Husayn (AS) as he fell on the sands of Karbala.
- **3:169** — "Do not think those slain in the way of Allah are dead; rather they live with their Lord, receiving provision" — the eternal life of the martyr of Ashura.
- **22:40** — Allah will surely aid those who aid His cause — Husayn (AS) gave everything so Allah's religion would not be erased; the message that outlives Yazid.

**Tafsir focus:** On the tenth of Muharram, after his sons, brothers and companions, Imam al-Husayn (AS) gave himself — the supreme sacrifice. Shia tradition reads over him the verses of the reassured soul returning to its Lord (89:27-30). The Quran's truth that the martyr is not dead but living and provided for (3:169) is realised in him most of all, and his stand preserved Allah's religion when it was nearest to being effaced (22:40). The lens is the eternity of Ashura: a defeat in the field that became, by the Quran's measure, the most enduring victory — every day Ashura, every land Karbala.

**Reflection:** Today the heart should break. Husayn (AS) gave his life so the religion of his grandfather ﷺ would live. Weep for him, and then ask: what in your life is now demanded for that same truth?
