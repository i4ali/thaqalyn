# Shia Islamic Events — Narrative Reference

Pre-researched scene structures for common events. Use these as starting points; always confirm with the user before generating.

All figures marked with ﷺ or ع have **veiled faces** (radiant white-gold halo) in images and videos. Never exceptions.

---

## Ghadeer Khumm (غدير خم)

- **Date:** 18 Dhul Hijjah, 10 AH / 19 March 632 CE
- **Tone:** Triumphant, declarative, sunset glory
- **Key figures:** Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, Imam Ali ع, 120,000+ pilgrims returning from Hajjat al-Wida
- **Canonical hadith:** "Man kuntu mawlahu fa-hadha Aliyun mawlah" — "Whoever I am his master, Ali is his master"
- **Canonical ayah:** Surah al-Maidah 5:67 (Balligh), 5:3 (Completion of religion)
- **Symbolic imagery:** Saddles stacked as a makeshift minbar, raised hands, setting sun, green + black banners, palm grove at oasis, camels and crowd

### Suggested 6-scene breakdown

1. **The caravan halts** — Wide shot: massive pilgrim caravan in desert at golden hour, camels silhouetted, dust in warm light. The command comes to stop at Ghadeer pond.
2. **Building the minbar** — Companions stacking camel saddles into a raised platform under the midday sun, palm trees around, anticipation in the crowd.
3. **The address begins** — Prophet ﷺ (veiled in light) ascends the saddle-minbar, crowd filling the valley, green banner behind him.
4. **The raising of the hand** — **CLIMAX**. Prophet ﷺ takes Imam Ali's ع hand and raises it high; both figures glow with halos, sun setting directly behind them, crowd stretches to horizon. *This is the image the user already has — match that reference.*
5. **The declaration** — Close-ish medium shot from behind the pilgrims looking up at the two raised figures; Arabic text overlay space for "من كنت مولاه فهذا علي مولاه".
6. **The congratulation (Tahniya)** — Umar ibn al-Khattab and companions stepping forward; text overlay of "Bakhin bakhin laka ya Ali" / "Congratulations, Ali, you have become my mawla and the mawla of every believer."

### Audio direction
Joyful. Arabic Ghadeer nasheed, "Ali Ali Ali Maula" tracks, Nasir al-Qatari, Mahmoud Ali Assaf.

## Ashura / Karbala (عاشوراء / کربلاء)

- **Date:** 10 Muharram 61 AH / 10 October 680 CE
- **Tone:** Solemn, tragic, reverent — never exploitative of grief
- **Key figures:** Imam Husayn ع, Abbas ع, Ali al-Akbar ع, Ali al-Asghar ع (infant), Zaynab س, Sakina, the 72 companions; opposing army of Yazid under Umar ibn Sa'd
- **Canonical:** "Kullu yawmin Ashura, kullu ardin Karbala"
- **Symbolic imagery:** Dry riverbed (Euphrates), parched earth, black banners, white horse (Zuljanah), water skins, red dust of Karbala, date palms, tents, arrows
- **Sensitivity:** Do not depict the moment of martyrdom graphically. Suggest through silhouette, empty armor, riderless horse, falling banner.

### Suggested 8-scene breakdown

1. **Arrival at Karbala** — 2 Muharram. Imam Husayn's ع caravan arriving at the barren plain, tents being pitched, Euphrates visible in distance.
2. **The siege and thirst** — 7 Muharram. Water cut off. Children with dry lips, water skins empty, Imam looking toward the river.
3. **The night of Ashura** — 9th night. Tents glowing from within with lamplight, companions reciting Quran, silhouettes praying, stars above.
4. **The morning stand** — Dawn of 10th. Imam ع in armor (face veiled in light), 72 companions behind him, the vast enemy army on the horizon under black banners of Yazid.
5. **Abbas and the water** — ع Abbas riding toward the Euphrates with water skin, reflection in water, reaching down.
6. **The infant Ali al-Asghar** — Imam ع holding the infant up in plea for water; white cloth. *Suggest the tragedy through composition, not explicit wound.*
7. **The final stand (zuhr)** — Imam ع alone in prayer at noon, sun directly overhead, arrows falling around him; pull wide to riderless Zuljanah returning to camp.
8. **The aftermath** — Tents burning at sunset, Zaynab س standing in silhouette facing the fire, dust rising; fade to black.

### Audio direction
Latmiyya. Basim al-Karbalai ("Hussain Hussain"), Mahmoud Karimi, Haj Mahmoud Kereimi, Farsi noha, Urdu soz. Slow, heavy. Never upbeat.

## Mubahala (مباهلة)

- **Date:** 24 Dhul Hijjah, 10 AH
- **Tone:** Triumphant, vindicating, confrontational
- **Key figures:** Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, Imam Ali ع, Fatima س, Hasan ع, Husayn ع (the Ahl al-Kisa); opposing: Christian delegation of Najran led by Bishop Abu Harithah
- **Canonical ayah:** Surah Aal-Imran 3:61
- **Symbolic imagery:** Five figures (all veiled) walking together at dawn, a black kisa (cloak), dramatic sky, the Christian delegation with their crosses and robes recoiling
- **Significance:** The Christians see divine light and refuse the challenge — prove the station of the Ahl al-Kisa.

### Suggested 5-scene breakdown

1. **The delegation arrives** — Najran Christians in their robes and crosses entering Medina under a theological confrontation.
2. **The debate at the mosque** — Inside Masjid al-Nabawi, Prophet ﷺ (veiled) seated, scholars of Najran debating, tension.
3. **The morning of Mubahala** — Dawn. The five figures (all veiled, all glowing) walking out of Medina toward the meeting place — Prophet leading, Ali behind, Fatima behind them, Hasan and Husayn at the sides, all under one black kisa or walking in formation.
4. **The encounter** — The Christians see the five, one bishop says "I see faces that if they asked God to move a mountain, He would move it." They refuse the curse.
5. **The return / pact of peace** — The Christians accepting jizya, the five returning to Medina, sun now fully risen behind them.

### Audio direction
Nasheed about Ahl al-Kisa, salawat chains, "Allahumma salli ala Muhammadin wa ale Muhammad." Hadith al-Kisa recitation overlay works beautifully here.

## Laylat al-Mabit (ليلة المبيت)

- **Date:** Night of 1 Rabi al-Awwal, 13th year of Prophethood / 622 CE
- **Tone:** Intimate, sacrificial, quiet heroism
- **Key figures:** Imam Ali ع (alone for most scenes), Prophet Muhammad ﷺ (briefly), the Quraysh assassins outside
- **Canonical ayah:** Surah al-Baqarah 2:207 "And among the people is he who sells his soul seeking the pleasure of Allah"
- **Symbolic imagery:** Night in Mecca, green Prophet's cloak covering a sleeping figure, moonlight through windows, Quraysh swords, dawn breaking

### Suggested 4-scene breakdown

1. **The plot** — Quraysh leaders conspiring in Dar al-Nadwa, swords being sharpened, plan to assassinate.
2. **The decision** — Inside Prophet's ﷺ home, lamplight, Imam Ali ع offering to sleep in the Prophet's bed. Prophet ﷺ leaves for Hijrah.
3. **The night vigil** — Low angle: Imam Ali ع under the green cloak in the Prophet's bed, silhouettes of assassins outside the door, moonlight, stillness.
4. **The dawn revelation** — First light. Assassins burst in with swords raised. Ali ع sits up — they recoil in shock. Sunrise through window.

### Audio direction
Quiet, reverent. Arabic instrumental (oud), recitation of ayah 2:207, munajat.

## Fath Khaybar (فتح خيبر)

- **Date:** Muharram 7 AH / 628 CE
- **Tone:** Valiant, martial
- **Key figures:** Prophet ﷺ, Imam Ali ع (hero), Marhab the Jewish champion of Khaybar
- **Canonical:** Prophet's ﷺ declaration "I will give the banner tomorrow to a man who loves Allah and His Messenger, and whom Allah and His Messenger love." "La fata illa Ali, la sayf illa Dhul-Fiqar."
- **Symbolic imagery:** The fortress of Khaybar (stone walls), the uprooted gate, Dhul-Fiqar sword, Prophet's white banner

### Suggested 5-scene breakdown

1. **The Muslim camp at dusk** — Previous commanders having failed. Prophet ﷺ announcing the banner-bearer of tomorrow.
2. **The handing of the banner** — Morning. Prophet ﷺ passing the white banner to Imam Ali ع; Ali's ع eyes had been treated by the Prophet's ﷺ saliva.
3. **The duel with Marhab** — Ali ع confronting the heavily armored Jewish champion outside the fortress.
4. **The gate of Khaybar** — Ali ع uprooting the massive iron gate and using it as a shield/bridge. Low hero angle.
5. **The conquest** — Muslims entering the fortress, white banner raised, sun at zenith.

### Audio direction
Martial nasheed. "La fata illa Ali" chants.

## Shahadat al-Zahra (شهادة الزهراء)

- **Date:** Jumada al-Thani / Rabi al-Thani 11 AH (3 or 6 months after Prophet's ﷺ passing)
- **Tone:** Grieving, heavy, quiet mourning
- **Sensitivity:** One of the most doctrinally charged events. Depict with dignity. Do not show the attack on the house explicitly — suggest through the burnt door, spilled contents, the broken rib.
- **Key figures:** Lady Fatima س (veiled), Imam Ali ع (veiled), Hasan ع, Husayn ع (children), Zaynab, Umm Kulthum — opposing: the mob at the door
- **Symbolic imagery:** The burnt door, the nail, a fallen veil, Medina by lamplight, an unmarked grave at night

### Suggested 6-scene breakdown

1. **After the Prophet** — Medina in mourning, Masjid al-Nabawi empty, Fatima's س house isolated.
2. **Fadak claimed and denied** — Fatima's س khutbah in the mosque (suggest through crowd, veiled figure standing, light from windows).
3. **The mob at the door** — Night. Torches, raised voices, the door of the house closing.
4. **The attack (suggested)** — Dark frame. The door cracks. A figure in black against the wall. *Do not depict the hit explicitly.* The scene cuts on sound.
5. **The recovery and decline** — Fatima س (veiled, in green or white) in bed, Ali ع at her side, the two young Imams holding her hand.
6. **The night burial** — Ali ع alone at night, torchlight, an unmarked grave. Medina sleeping. The moon setting.

### Audio direction
Deepest latmiyya. Noha for Zahra. Very slow. Silence is powerful here — consider long pauses.

## Eid al-Mubahala / 24 Dhul Hijjah ring incident

- **Date:** 24 Dhul Hijjah, during the Caliphate of Ali ع or earlier — Imam Ali ع gives his ring in sajda
- **Canonical ayah:** Surah al-Maidah 5:55 (Ayat al-Wilayah)
- **Tone:** Intimate, pious
- **Symbolic imagery:** A beggar entering the mosque, rows of prayer, a raised hand from sajda offering a ring, dust motes in shafts of light

### 4-scene breakdown

1. The beggar enters Masjid al-Nabawi, asks for help, everyone silent.
2. Wide shot: rows of worshippers in sajda, one figure (Ali ع, veiled) at the end.
3. Close: the hand from sajda extended, ring being lifted off the finger.
4. The beggar leaves with the ring; rays of light; Gabriel (not depicted — suggested through light) bringing the ayah down.

### Audio direction
Quran recitation of 5:55, quiet nasheed, nay/oud.

## How to add a new event

When the user mentions an event not listed here, don't guess. Either:

1. Research it briefly (web search if a search tool is available, otherwise ask the user for the key narrative beats and sources they trust).
2. Propose a draft structure following the same format (date, tone, figures, symbolic imagery, scene breakdown, audio).
3. Get explicit confirmation before generating images.

Common events worth adding: Wiladat (births) of the 12 Imams, Shahadat (martyrdoms) of each Imam, the Mi'raj, the Isra, Bay'at al-Ridwan, the Conquest of Mecca, the Farewell Pilgrimage, the battles of Badr / Uhud / Khandaq / Siffin / Jamal / Nahrawan, the ambassadorship of Muslim ibn Aqeel, Arbaeen, the return of the Ahl al-Bayt to Medina.
