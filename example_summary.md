# Uplifting Ayah Example

## Surah Az-Zumar, Ayah 53

### Arabic Text

قُلْ يَـٰعِبَادِىَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَسْرَفُوا۟ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمْ لَا تَقْنَطُوا۟ مِن رَّحْمَةِ ٱللَّهِ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَغْفِرُ ٱلذُّنُوبَ جَمِيعًا ۚ إِنَّهُۥ هُوَ ٱلْغَفُورُ ٱلرَّحِيمُ

### Translation

"Say, 'O My servants who have transgressed against themselves [by sinning], do not despair of the mercy of Allah. Indeed, Allah forgives all sins. Indeed, it is He who is the Forgiving, the Merciful.'"

### Tafsir Summary

In Tafsir al-Mizan, Allama Tabatabai explains that this verse is one of the most comforting messages in the Qur'an, as it extends hope to those who feel burdened by their sins.

Allah is speaking directly to those who may have strayed, reassuring them that they should not despair of His mercy. The promise that Allah forgives all sins highlights the vastness of His forgiveness and His desire to bring people back to His mercy, no matter how far they may feel.

This verse encourages believers to seek repentance with the assurance that Allah's mercy is greater than any sin.
